# Quick Google Classroom Tutorial
# Source: https://vimeo.com/733707304/3544b81032

Speaker A: Google Classroom is a digital learning space that is designed to help your instructor communicate and collaborate with you. It will also help you stay organized, complete assignments and successfully finish the course. To login, go to classroom.google.com or click on the array of squares at the top of the Google homepage. Please make sure that your Google account is the one that we've sent you an invitation to because that's the one you will be using for your Google Classroom and all the other course materials. Lets look at how to navigate Google Classroom Click on the menu lines in the top left of your screen. In this menu you will see the following Classes will take you to the classroom homepage Calendar will show you a calendar view of all assignments to do will show you all the upcoming assignments that have been assigned by your instructor and a list of classes that you are currently enrolled in. The stream is where you can view all of the notifications, announcements, assignments, comments and discussion topics if enabled by the instructor. You can also add post and leave comments. Use the Announce something to your class box to post something to the entire classroom. You can also use the buttons below to attach files, links and videos. Click Post to add this to the class stream. The Classwork tab is where you will spend most of your time in Google Classroom. All materials, questions and assignments will appear on this page. The first you join Google Classroom you will see the Starter Pack. It's a place that has everything you need to prepare for the course start. Content of the Starter Pack differs from course to course depending on the topic and audience. However, a few files are always there. Course Program contains all the information needed to pass the course successfully. Class Schedule to not miss a class office hours schedule to ask content related questions and the grade for each assignment. If you have any question regarding course logistics, please check out the faq. Here's a quick guide on how you can turn an assignment so that your instructor can see, review and grade them. Expectation form folder has a link that takes you straight to the form. Please take a moment to fill it in for us to get to know your better. Below you will see folders for each class. Class Recording posted the day after the lesson. Each video is protected with a password displayed directly above the link to the video. The zoom link for each class will be waiting for you in classwork two hours before the start of class. The link will also be sent in Google Classroom stream 10 minutes before the lesson. Class slides are posted either before or after class. You can always go back and review them. Resources is the folder where you will find all the Additional Course Materials Articles Videos Case Studies the Assignment folder is usually posted right after class. It contains a description of the work that you are to do at home. Clicking on a box will expand to show additional information. Clicking on View Assignment will open the Assignment page and will show you the point value and due date. If your instructor has attached a template for you to open and complete, you will see it in the top right corner in the youe Work box. If this box is blank, you will use the Add or Create button. This will let you attach a link or file from your computer or your Google Drive. This button will also let you create a new file directly in classroom such as a doc, slide sheet or drawing. Be careful. Once you have turned in an assignment, you will no longer be able to edit the document. Once turned in, your instructor will grade it and return it with feedback. The View your work link is a quick and easy way to see all assignments that your instructor has sent to you. Also, you can use it to view the grades that you received for each assignment. We hope this quick guide will help you easily navigate Google Classroom and find all the materials you need for the course. See you in class.
