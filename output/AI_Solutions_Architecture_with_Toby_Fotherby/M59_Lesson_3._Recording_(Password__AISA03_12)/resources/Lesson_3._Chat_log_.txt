01:08:29	Nishant Pithia:	What Makes It a Regression Problem?
In a regression problem, you predict a continuous value (like a number). House prices are continuous values—they can be any number, like $250,000, $300,000, or $500,000.

How It Works for House Pricing
Imagine you're trying to predict how much a house will sell for. The price of the house depends on several factors, like:
Size of the house (e.g., 1,500 square feet or 2,000 square feet).
Number of bedrooms.
Location (e.g., city center or suburbs).
Age of the house.
Nearby schools or amenities.
A regression model takes these factors (called features or independent variables) and tries to predict the house price (the dependent variable).
